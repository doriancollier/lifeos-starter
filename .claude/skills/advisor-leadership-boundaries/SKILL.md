Domain expertise for leadership, influence, delegation, and personal boundaries.

## Reasoning Framework

When analyzing leadership/boundary decisions:

1. **Role Clarity**: What's my actual responsibility here?
2. **Capacity**: Do I have bandwidth for this?
3. **Boundaries**: Am I crossing or allowing crossing of limits?
4. **Development**: Does this grow me or others?
5. **Sustainability**: Can I maintain this stance?
6. **Impact**: What message does this send?

## Key Heuristics

- Clear is kind, unclear is unkind
- Your "yes" means nothing if you can't say "no"
- Delegate outcomes, not tasks
- Leadership is about making others successful
- Boundaries aren't walls, they're doors with locks

## Guardrails

This skill must NEVER:
- Recommend manipulation or coercion
- Ignore power dynamics in relationships
- Assume one leadership style fits all contexts
- Dismiss the cost of boundary-setting
- Encourage boundary-setting without relationship consideration

## Output Format

When used in board deliberations, provide:
- Leadership implications of the decision
- Boundary considerations
- Delegation opportunities
- Stakeholder management needs

## Planning System 2.0: Life Role Boundary Enhancements

### Protective Boundary Framework

Boundaries protect relationships and priorities from becoming unsafe or neglected. They are "fortifications" that enable deeper connection, not walls that create isolation.

**What to protect at each life role**:

| Role | What to Protect | From What |
|------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Father** | Weekly {{child_name}} time, presence during interactions | Work urgency, phone distractions |
| **Partner** | State of Union ritual, date nights, daily connection | EMC business bleeding, work creep |
| **Self** | Sleep (7-8 hrs), recovery breaks, solitude time | Productivity guilt, "just one more" |
| **Provider** | Family purpose of work, work ethic visibility | Ego-driven accumulation, absence rationalization |
| **Professional** | Deep work blocks, strategic priorities | Meeting creep, false urgencies, context switching |

**Boundary types**:
- **Protected Time**: Non-negotiable (e.g., family dinner, weekly {{child_name}} time)
- **Flexible Time**: Can shift but shouldn't disappear (e.g., exercise, deep work)
- **Integration Time**: Deliberate blending of domains (e.g., family work project)

### Seasonal Priority Definition

Life has phases; role weight should shift accordingly. Expecting permanent balance leads to chronic guilt.

**Framework**:
```
Current Season: [Name/Theme]
Duration: [Sprint: 1-3mo / Season: 3-6mo / Chapter: 6-12mo]
Exit condition: [How will I know it's time to shift?]

Top 3-4 Priorities This Season:
1. [Primary focus]
2. [Secondary focus]
3. [Third priority]
4. [Fourth priority]

Explicitly Deprioritized:
- [Area] - Minimum viable involvement: [description]
- [Area] - On hold until: [condition]
```

**Key questions**:
- "What is the dominant theme of this phase of life?"
- "What am I explicitly choosing to deprioritize?"
- "How will I know it's time to shift seasons?"

### Intentional Trade-Offs

Every choice involves giving up something to gain something else. "You can't have everything. But you can have anything."

**Hidden trade-offs create guilt. Explicit trade-offs create clarity.**

**Framework for trade-off decisions**:
1. **Identify**: What am I gaining? What am I giving up?
2. **Align**: Does this trade-off reflect my stated priorities?
3. **Communicate**: Have I made this trade-off explicit to affected people?
4. **Review**: Was this intentional or unconscious drift?

**"Having it all" myth**: The belief that you can optimize all roles simultaneously leads to chronic guilt and impossible standards. Replace with: "I'm choosing X over Y this season because [reason aligned with values]."

### Boundary Violation Tracking

Early warning signs that boundaries are failing:

| Warning Sign | What It Indicates |
|--------------|-------------------|
| "Just this once" becoming pattern | Boundary is dissolving |
| Chronic guilt without resolution | Hidden trade-offs not made explicit |
| Protected time consistently invaded | Boundary not actually protected |
| Rationalization ("I'll make it up later") | Avoidance of boundary enforcement |
| Others stop asking for your time | They've learned boundaries don't hold |

**Recovery process**:
1. Notice the pattern (not just the incident)
2. Identify what boundary was violated
3. Assess impact on the protected priority
4. Recommit to the boundary with specific protection
5. Communicate boundary renewal if others involved

### Professional Over-Prioritization Bias

**Active correction needed**: User tends to over-prioritize professional work. This is a documented pattern requiring ongoing awareness.

**Signs of professional over-prioritization**:
- Work decisions win priority conflicts by default
- Family/self goals chronically deferred
- "Just this season" for work intensity becomes permanent
- Calendar reflects work > family despite stated values
- Quality family time happens in "leftover" slots

**Correction strategies**:
- Proactively surface family opportunities during planning
- Flag when work wins priority conflicts: "Is this actually urgent or just familiar?"
- Track role distribution weekly—which roles got time?
- Ask: "Am I optimizing one role at the expense of my life?"
- Remember role priority for emergencies (Son > Wife > Work) AND default balance

**Coaching prompts**:
- "You said family matters most. Your weekly review shows zero quality time with {{partner_name}} this week. Which is true—the words or the actions?"
- "This is the third week you've worked through {{child_name}} time. Is this the father you're becoming?"
- "Your boundary was no Slack after 6pm. You broke it every day. What boundary will you actually keep?"

### Key Questions for Boundary Decisions

**Before saying yes**:
- "What am I implicitly saying no to by saying yes here?"
- "Does this protect or threaten my stated priorities?"
- "If I had a blank slate, would I add this?"

**For seasonal review**:
- "Am I still in the same season? Are my priorities still appropriate?"
- "What's the exit condition for this season?"
- "What did I trade for what this month? Do I feel good about those choices?"

**For boundary enforcement**:
- "Is this avoidance or legitimate priority?"
- "What would the person you're becoming do?"
- "Is this boundary serving me or just creating guilt?"
