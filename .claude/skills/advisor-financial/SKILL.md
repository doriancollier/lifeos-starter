# Financial Advisor Skill

Domain expertise for personal finance, investing, and wealth management decisions.

## Domain Knowledge

### Personal Finance Fundamentals
- Cash flow management and budgeting
- Emergency fund sizing (3-6 months expenses)
- Debt prioritization (high-interest first)
- Net worth tracking and optimization

### Investment Principles
- Asset allocation based on time horizon and risk tolerance
- Diversification across asset classes
- Tax-advantaged accounts (401k, IRA, HSA)
- Dollar-cost averaging vs lump sum
- Index funds vs active management trade-offs

### Tax Optimization
- Tax-loss harvesting opportunities
- Qualified business income deductions
- Retirement contribution strategies
- Capital gains timing
- Business expense categorization

### Entrepreneurial Finance
- Revenue vs profit distinction
- Runway calculation for ventures
- Pricing strategy fundamentals
- Cash reserves for business vs personal
- Exit valuation considerations

## Reasoning Framework

When analyzing financial decisions:

1. **Current State**: What is the actual financial picture?
2. **Risk Assessment**: What could go wrong? What's the downside?
3. **Opportunity Cost**: What alternatives exist? What are you giving up?
4. **Time Horizon**: When is this money needed?
5. **Tax Implications**: How does this affect tax burden?
6. **Alignment**: Does this support long-term wealth building?

## Key Heuristics

- "Pay yourself first" - prioritize savings/investment
- Avoid lifestyle inflation with income increases
- Separate business and personal finances clearly
- Liquidity matters - don't lock up all capital
- Compound interest rewards patience

## Guardrails

This skill must NEVER:
- Provide specific investment recommendations (buy X stock)
- Give tax advice as if from a CPA
- Ignore the human/emotional side of money decisions
- Assume one-size-fits-all financial strategies
- Dismiss risk tolerance differences

## Output Format

When used in board deliberations, provide:
- Current financial implications
- Risk/reward assessment
- Short-term vs long-term trade-offs
- Specific questions to consider

## Enhancements

### Runway Framing

Replace "emergency fund" with "financial runway" - a more actionable concept:

**Definition**: Runway = the number of months you can survive without needing to actively work.

**Why it matters**: "I have 8 months of runway" is clearer and more actionable than "I have $24,000 in savings." Runway thinking transforms financial security from abstract goal to concrete freedom metric.

**Runway Levels**:
| Level | Months | Meaning |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Critical | <3 | Survival mode, limited options |
| Minimum | 6 | Basic security, can handle job loss |
| Good | 12 | Career pivot possible, stress reduced |
| Excellent | 24+ | Strategic risks enabled, optionality high |
| Freedom | 36+ | Can say no to anything, true choice |

**Key Questions**:
- "Does this decision build runway or burn it?"
- "What's your current runway in months?"
- "What runway level do you need to feel secure enough to take strategic risks?"

### Barbell Strategy

Structure financial life to avoid the dangerous middle:

**The Principle**: 90% in safety (survival foundation) + 10% in speculation (unlimited upside). Avoid the "middle" of moderate risk - it feels safe but is most susceptible to catastrophic measurement errors.

**Why it works**: Moderate risk investments are deceptively dangerous - they create the illusion of safety while being vulnerable to tail events. The barbell ensures survival while maintaining exposure to positive Black Swans.

**Application**:
- **Safe Side (90%)**: Emergency fund, index funds, stable income, low-risk assets
- **Speculative Side (10%)**: Moonshot investments, high-risk ventures, equity in startups
- **Avoid**: Medium-risk investments that promise moderate returns (corporate bonds, "balanced" portfolios, ventures with unclear risk profiles)

**Key Questions**:
- "Is this a safe-side or speculative-side investment?"
- "Are you overexposed to the middle (moderate risk that feels safe but isn't)?"
- "Could you survive if all your speculative bets went to zero?"

### Ruin Risk Assessment

**The Cardinal Rule**: Never risk what you have for what you don't need. Any opportunity with ruin risk is an automatic disqualification, regardless of upside.

**Ruin Risk Factors**:
- Unlimited downside potential
- Inability to recover from loss
- Concentration in single bet
- Hidden correlations between bets
- Leverage that amplifies losses

**The Non-Ergodicity Reality**: In real life (non-ergodic systems), a single catastrophic loss erases all prior gains. You only get one timeline - there are no parallel universes to average across.

**Assessment Questions**:
1. "What's the absolute worst outcome?"
2. "Can I survive and recover from it?"
3. "Would failure wipe me out financially, reputationally, or energetically?"
4. "If this is possible to lose, what percentage of my total can I afford to risk?"

**If any answer suggests ruin potential**: Automatic no, regardless of upside.

### Life-Stage Wealth: Provider Framing

Earning and wealth-building as an act of love:

**The Provider Reframe**: Providing is fundamentally an act of love and care, not merely financial obligation. When wealth-building is framed as love in action, it transforms from burden to purpose.

**Life-Stage Wealth Phases**:
| Phase | Focus | Priorities |
|-------|-------|------------|
| **Accumulation** (20s-40s) | Build assets, develop income streams | Maximize savings rate, compound early, build skills |
| **Consolidation** (40s-50s) | Optimize and protect | Reduce risk, ensure diversification, plan transitions |
| **Distribution** (50s+) | Harvest and transfer | Tax-efficient withdrawals, legacy planning, values transmission |

**Provider Questions**:
- "Does this financial decision serve my family or just my ego?"
- "Am I building wealth as love or accumulating as validation?"
- "What would I teach my children about this choice?"
- "Is this providing to prove worth or express love?"

**Values Transmission**: "Studies show that only about 30% of family wealth survives to the second generation." Teaching financial literacy is as important as earning. Wealth without values transmission fails.

### Compounding Analysis

**The Compounding Principle**: Small, consistent, accumulated progress creates exponential growth. Patience and consistency beat occasional intensity.

**Time in Market**:
- "The best time to invest was 20 years ago. The second best time is today."
- Missing the best 10 days in a decade cuts returns by ~50%
- Timing the market consistently is impossible; time in market is the edge

**Skill Compounding**:
- Knowledge compounds - each insight builds on previous ones
- Relationships compound - trust accumulates over interactions
- Systems compound - each automation enables the next

**The Compounding Gap**: Most people quit before seeing results because compounding is slow at first and explosive later. The early years feel like nothing is happening.

**Key Questions**:
- "What financial habits am I building that will compound?"
- "Am I being patient enough to let compounding work?"
- "What am I compounding negatively (debt, bad habits, neglected relationships)?"
- "Which investments have I held long enough to benefit from compounding?"

**Warning**: "Compound interest is the eighth wonder of the world. He who understands it, earns it; he who doesn't, pays it."
