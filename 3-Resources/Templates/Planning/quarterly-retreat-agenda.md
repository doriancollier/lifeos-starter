# Quarterly Retreat Agenda: {{year}} Q{{quarter}}

> "Physical separation creates psychological space for strategic thinking."

This agenda structures a 1-2 day personal retreat for quarterly transition. Book all four retreats for the year in advance. Location should be within 2 hours, in nature, different from your usual workspace.

---

## Pre-Retreat Preparation

### One Week Before

- [ ] Location confirmed and booked
- [ ] Travel arrangements made
- [ ] Calendar cleared of commitments
- [ ] Out-of-office set (if needed)
- [ ] Materials gathered:
  - [ ] [[quarterly-review]] template (printed or digital)
  - [ ] [[quarterly-plan]] template
  - [ ] Last quarter's plan for reference
  - [ ] [[annual-plan]] for alignment check
  - [ ] Journal or notebook
  - [ ] Comfortable clothes

### Day Before

- [ ] Devices charged
- [ ] Non-essential notifications disabled
- [ ] Family/team informed of unavailability
- [ ] Any urgent items delegated or addressed
- [ ] Intention set for the retreat

## Evening Arrival (Day 0)

**Duration:** 2-3 hours

### Unwind and Settle (60 min)

- Arrive, unpack, get comfortable
- Take a walk to transition mentally
- Light dinner — nothing heavy
- No work, no screens

### Opening Reflection (30 min)

**Prompt:** Sit quietly and ask yourself:
- How am I feeling right now?
- What am I carrying into this retreat?
- What do I hope to leave with?

Write freely for 15-20 minutes without editing.

### Set Intentions (15 min)

**My intention for this retreat:**


**What I want to be true by the time I leave:**


### Evening Ritual

- Light reading (nothing work-related)
- Early to bed (protect tomorrow's energy)
- No screens 1 hour before sleep

---

## Day 1: Review and Reflect

### Morning Ritual (30 min)

- Wake naturally (no alarm if possible)
- Light movement or stretching
- Quiet breakfast, no devices
- Brief meditation or journaling

### Deep Quarterly Review (3-4 hours)

**Use the [[quarterly-review]] template**

Work through each section thoughtfully. Don't rush. Take breaks as needed.

**Suggested pacing:**
- Part 1: Priority Progress (45 min)
- Part 2: Lead Indicator Analysis (30 min)
- Part 3: Domain Balance (30 min)
- *Short break — walk outside*
- Part 4: Energy Patterns (30 min)
- Part 5: Learnings and Adjustments (45 min)
- Part 6: Annual Goal Check (30 min)
- Part 7: Wins and Gratitude (15 min)
- Part 8: Forward Look (15 min)

### Midday Break (2-3 hours)

**This break is essential, not optional.**

- Long walk in nature
- Lunch (light, energizing)
- Nap if needed
- Let insights percolate
- No structured thinking — let the mind wander

### Integration Session (60-90 min)

After the break, review your quarterly review notes and capture:

4.
5.

**What I'm Most Proud Of:**


**What I Need to Let Go Of:**


**The Pattern I Most Need to Break:**


**The Strength I Most Need to Leverage:**


### Evening Transition

- Light dinner
- Journaling or creative activity
- No planning yet — let review insights settle
- Early rest

---

## Day 2: Plan and Commit

### Morning Ritual (30 min)

- Same as Day 1
- Start with gratitude: What am I grateful for after yesterday's reflection?

### Quarterly Planning (2-3 hours)

**Use the [[quarterly-plan]] template**

Work through each section. Reference your review insights and annual plan.

**Suggested pacing:**
- Annual Alignment Check (15 min)
- Quarterly Priorities — First Draft (60 min)
- *Short break*
- What I'm Saying NO To (15 min)
- Implementation Intentions / WOOP (30 min)
- Weekly Breakdown (30 min)
- Support Structures (15 min)

### Midday Break (60-90 min)

- Walk and process
- Light lunch
- Let the plan settle before finalizing

### Finalization and Commitment (60 min)

**Review your draft plan:**

- Are the priorities truly the MOST important things?
- Are there only 3-5? If more, what gets cut?
- Does each priority have clear key results?
- Are the weekly lead indicators specific and actionable?
- Is this plan realistic given your energy and commitments?

**Stress test the plan:**

- What happens if [likely disruption] occurs?
- Which priority would I drop if I had to?
- Does this plan protect family time?
- Does this plan include recovery?

**Finalize and commit:**

- Update any priority wording
- Complete the commitment statement
- Sign the plan

### Closing Ritual (30 min)

**Integration Questions:**

What am I taking away from this retreat?


What will be different because of this time?


What's my first action when I return?


**Gratitude:**

What am I grateful for about this time?


**Closing Statement:**

I leave this retreat committed to [one sentence summary of Q{{quarter}} focus].

## Abbreviated Alternative: Half-Day Retreat

If a full retreat isn't possible, here's a condensed version (4-5 hours):

### Morning (3 hours)

1. Opening reflection (15 min)
2. Abbreviated quarterly review — focus on:
   - Priority progress assessment (30 min)
   - Key learnings and patterns (30 min)
   - Energy audit (15 min)
3. Break / walk (30 min)
4. Quarterly planning — focus on:
   - 3 priorities with key results (45 min)
   - Implementation intentions (15 min)

### Afternoon (1-2 hours)

1. Finalization and commitment (30 min)
2. Closing ritual (15 min)
3. Post-retreat actions capture (15 min)
